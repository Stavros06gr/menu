Safety Systems

Vehicle safety systems are designed to protect passengers and reduce the risk of injury during accidents. They include features such as airbags, seatbelts, ABS, traction control, and advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS).

How Safety Systems Work

  • Seatbelts: Keep occupants restrained during sudden stops or collisions.
  • Airbags: Deploy on impact to cushion and protect passengers.
  • ABS (Anti-lock Braking System): Prevents wheel lockup during braking.
  • Traction Control: Helps maintain grip on slippery surfaces.

Key Components

  • Airbags: Front, side, curtain, and knee airbags protect various parts of the body.
  • Seatbelt Pretensioners: Tighten seatbelts instantly during a crash.
  • Electronic Stability Control (ESC): Helps maintain vehicle control.
  • ADAS: Includes lane assist, automatic braking, blind-spot monitoring, and more.
