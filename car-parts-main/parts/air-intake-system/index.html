Air Intake System

The air intake system is responsible for bringing clean and measured amounts of air into the engine for the combustion process. It includes components that filter, measure, and guide air into the intake manifold and engine cylinders, ensuring the right air-to-fuel ratio for efficiency and performance.

Mechanical Process

  • Air Filter: Removes dust, debris, and contaminants from incoming air before it reaches the engine.
  • Mass Air Flow Sensor (MAF): Measures the amount of air entering the system to help the ECU determine the correct fuel delivery.
  • Throttle Body: Regulates the amount of air that flows into the engine, controlled by the accelerator pedal or ECU.
  • Intake Manifold: Distributes the incoming air evenly to each cylinder for optimal combustion.

Core Components

  • Air Filter Housing: Holds the air filter and connects to the intake piping.
  • MAF Sensor: Provides airflow data to the ECU for proper fuel mixture adjustment.
  • Throttle Plate: A butterfly valve that opens and closes to control airflow.
  • Manifold: Channels air from the throttle body to the intake valves of each cylinder.
