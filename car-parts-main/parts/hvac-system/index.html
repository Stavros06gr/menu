HVAC System

The HVAC system controls the temperature, humidity, and air quality inside the vehicle. It includes components like the compressor, condenser, evaporator, heater core, and blower motor.

How It Works

  • Compressor: Pressurizes refrigerant and starts the cooling cycle.
  • Condenser: Releases heat from the refrigerant to the outside air.
  • Evaporator: Absorbs heat from inside the cabin for cooling.
  • Heater Core: Transfers engine heat to warm the cabin air.

Key Components

  • Compressor: The heart of the A/C system, circulates refrigerant.
  • Condenser: Located at the front, cools high-pressure gas into liquid.
  • Evaporator: Cools and dehumidifies air entering the cabin.
  • Blower Motor: Pushes air through the HVAC ducts.
